## Summary

Aniketh Dsouza (retail trader, marketing background, no coding) demonstrates a "second brain" system built on Claude Code + Obsidian to learn and apply Mark Minervini's VCP/trend-template methodology faster. The core idea: ingest all learning materials (YouTube transcripts, PDFs, GitHub repos, images) into an offline Obsidian vault, use Claude Code as the reasoning engine over that vault, and automate daily stock scanning via slash commands and Telegram alerts. The video is not about options but contains directly portable AI workflow architecture and trade-analytics techniques.

---

## Strategies

- **Minervini Trend Template (8-point checklist):** Price above 50/150/200 DMA, 200 DMA trending up, 50 > 150 > 200, price > 52w low by 30%+, RS rating threshold. Used as a pass/fail gate before any position.
- **VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern):** Series of price contractions with decreasing volume on down legs, buy on breakout with tight stop. Learned offline from ingested material (books, transcripts, PDFs) rather than live web search.
- **Tightness filter for stock selection:** Last 3 candles must have high-to-low range ≤ 6%. Used to filter Minervini list down to actionable candidates daily.
- **IPO age filter:** Stocks with IPO < 10 years flagged separately as higher-growth candidates within the Minervini list.

---

## Metrics & Ratios

- **Trade edge calculation via data dump:** Export broker console data (Zerodha) → paste into Claude → get win rate, avg win, avg loss, edge ratio, and a forward projection of how long to reach a capital target at current performance. Surfaced that at ₹5k/trade max loss, reaching ₹1Cr target would take 7 years — forcing a position-sizing rethink.
- **RS Rating from MarketSmith India:** Fetched programmatically via session-ID scraping; used as a filter alongside trend template.
- **Relative Strength Line:** Tracked visually on TradingView via a custom Chrome extension built with Claude Code.

## Risk Management

- Max loss per trade set in absolute terms (₹5,000 during learning phase) — speaker concluded this was too small relative to capital goal and needed revision.
- Position sizing evaluated by projecting time-to-goal: if the math says 7 years, the system is broken — change the system, not the goal.
- No options-specific risk management discussed.

---

## Journal / Process

- **Self-awareness prompt:** "Take my last N months of trade exports from [broker] and tell me: win rate, avg win, avg loss, edge ratio. Then tell me how long it takes to reach ₹X at this rate." — Forces quantitative self-assessment without manual journal work.
- **Three S's framework (speaker's own):** Strategy (what everyone teaches) → Setup (fewer teach) → System (fewest teach). Real consistency only comes at the System level — treating trading as a repeatable business process.
- **Death by a thousand cuts** as the pattern to break: jumping between strategies without measuring which works, leading to accumulation of small losses with no learning.
- **Compounding knowledge:** Obsidian vault as a living knowledge graph — each new source increments the graph, old contradicted nodes get replaced. The vault represents accumulated trader knowledge that can be copied and handed to anyone.
- **Learning speed:** The second brain collapses the traditional 3-5 year learning cycle by allowing parallel ingestion and instant retrieval vs. sequential reading and manual note-taking.
